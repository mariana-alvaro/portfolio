Hi! 👋 I'm Mariana Alvaro, a Venezuelan Computer Science-Economics student at Brown University.



I was born in Colombia, grew up in Venezuela and Panama, and currently study in Providence. Growing up in Venezuela, I witnessed how the economy was getting weaker every day, and the many consequences that that implied. Thus, I began to understand from an early age the importance of Economics and became very interested in it.

It was only at Brown that I became interested in CS, after taking the intro class because one of my friends was a Teaching Assistant (TA). It was, and continues to be, very challenging for me, but I like how it allows me to create content and communicate my ideas. I still have a long way to go, but I'm excited to embark on this journey.

At Brown, I'm a TA for the Essential Math for Economics class, an intern at the Office of Sustainability, and the Finance Chair of the Brown International Scholarship Committee.

Outside of Brown, singing and occasionally song writing🎶, drawing and painting, checking the stock market, and going outdoors whenever it's sunny 🌞.
